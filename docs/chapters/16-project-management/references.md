# References: IS Project Management

1. [Project management](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Project_management) - Wikipedia - Comprehensive overview of project management process groups, knowledge areas, and methodologies. Anchors the chapter's PM framing.

2. [Scrum (software development)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Scrum_(software_development)) - Wikipedia - Detailed coverage of Scrum roles, events, and artifacts. Directly supports the chapter's Scrum sprint-cycle MicroSim.

3. [Earned value management](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Earned_value_management) - Wikipedia - Clear treatment of EVM with worked examples of CV, SV, CPI, and SPI calculations. Foundation for the chapter's EVM content.

4. PMBOK Guide (7th Edition) - Project Management Institute - PMI - The authoritative reference for project management aligned with the PMP certification; provides the structured vocabulary and methodology this chapter teaches.

5. The Scrum Guide - Ken Schwaber and Jeff Sutherland - Scrum.org - The official Scrum framework definition; concise enough that students should read the whole thing alongside this chapter.

6. [Scrum Guide Official Site](https://scrumguides.org/) - Schwaber and Sutherland - Free official Scrum Guide in 30+ languages. The single source of truth for Scrum events and roles taught in this chapter.

7. [Atlassian Agile Coach](https://www.atlassian.com/agile) - Atlassian - Vendor-neutral practitioner content on Scrum, Kanban, and SAFe with concrete examples. Excellent companion to the academic content.

8. [SAFe Framework](https://scaledagileframework.com/) - Scaled Agile, Inc. - Comprehensive reference for the Scaled Agile Framework used in large enterprise IS programs. Required vocabulary for many enterprise IS roles.

9. [PRINCE2 Methodology](https://www.axelos.com/certifications/propath/prince2-project-management/prince2-foundation-and-practitioner) - AXELOS - Reference for the PRINCE2 method widely used in UK/EU government and enterprise contexts. Useful contrast to PMBOK and Scrum.

10. [Gantt Chart Tutorial](https://www.smartsheet.com/gantt-chart-explanation) - Smartsheet - Practical guide to Gantt charts and critical path method. Directly supports the chapter's Gantt-chart MicroSim.
