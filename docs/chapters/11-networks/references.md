# References: Networks and Telecommunications for Business

1. [Computer network](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Computer_network) - Wikipedia - Comprehensive overview of LAN, WAN, MAN, and internet concepts at the level appropriate for IS managers. Anchors the chapter's network framing.

2. [Software-defined wide area network](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/SD-WAN) - Wikipedia - Detailed coverage of SD-WAN technology, vendors, and the migration from MPLS that drives modern enterprise networking. Directly relevant to chapter procurement content.

3. [Service-level agreement](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Service-level_agreement) - Wikipedia - Clear treatment of SLA structure, metrics, and penalties used in telecom contracts. Anchors the chapter's procurement section.

4. Computer Networking: A Top-Down Approach (8th Edition) - James F. Kurosawa and Keith W. Ross - Pearson - The dominant academic networking textbook; chapters on application, transport, and network layers provide the depth IS managers need without the protocol-engineering deep dive.

5. Business Data Communications and Networking (14th Edition) - Jerry FitzGerald, Alan Dennis, and Alexandra Durcikova - Wiley - Manager-oriented treatment of business networking, capacity planning, and telecom procurement; directly aligned with this chapter's depth.

6. [Cisco Networking Basics](https://www.cisco.com/c/en/us/solutions/small-business/resource-center/networking/networking-basics.html) - Cisco - Vendor-neutral introduction to networking concepts at IS-manager level, with practical examples from real enterprise deployments.

7. [Cloudflare Learning Center](https://www.cloudflare.com/learning/) - Cloudflare - Free educational content on networking, DNS, CDNs, VPNs, and zero-trust networking concepts referenced throughout this chapter.

8. [What is a VPN?](https://www.cloudflare.com/learning/access-management/what-is-a-vpn/) - Cloudflare Learning - Clear explanation of VPN types (site-to-site, remote access) and their role in modern enterprise architecture. Pairs with the chapter's enterprise-network MicroSim.

9. [Bandwidth, Latency, and Throughput](https://www.fastly.com/learning/cdn/the-difference-between-throughput-and-bandwidth) - Fastly - Concise explanation of the three core network metrics with worked examples. Directly supports the chapter's bandwidth/latency MicroSim.

10. [Telecom Procurement Best Practices](https://www.gartner.com/en/information-technology/glossary/telecommunications-services) - Gartner - Industry analyst perspective on enterprise telecom procurement, vendor selection, and capacity planning. Reinforces the chapter's procurement content.
