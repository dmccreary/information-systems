# References: AI Law, Regulation, and Compliance

1. [Artificial Intelligence Act](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Artificial_Intelligence_Act) - Wikipedia - Comprehensive coverage of the EU AI Act including risk tiers, prohibited practices, and high-risk obligations. Anchors the chapter's regulatory framing.

2. [Regulation of artificial intelligence](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Regulation_of_artificial_intelligence) - Wikipedia - Detailed survey of global AI regulatory approaches across the EU, US, UK, China, and emerging frameworks. Foundation for the chapter's comparative-law content.

3. [Equal Credit Opportunity Act](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Equal_Credit_Opportunity_Act) - Wikipedia - Coverage of ECOA and its application to AI-driven lending decisions, including adverse action notice requirements. Supports the sector-specific compliance content.

4. The Oxford Handbook of AI Governance - Justin B. Bullock et al. (eds.) - Oxford University Press - Comprehensive academic reference on AI governance regimes; useful depth supplement when chapters need a particular regulation cited authoritatively.

5. The Regulation of Artificial Intelligence - Tambiama Madiega and EPRS - Springer - Practitioner-focused reference on the implementation of AI law across jurisdictions; directly aligned with the chapter's comparative-regulation outcomes.

6. [EU AI Act Official Text](https://artificialintelligenceact.eu/) - Future of Life Institute - Searchable, well-cross-referenced version of the EU AI Act with explanatory commentary. The single most useful resource for the chapter's regulatory content.

7. [NIST AI Resource Center](https://airc.nist.gov/) - NIST - US federal hub for AI guidance including the AI RMF, generative AI profile, and emerging US federal AI policy. Authoritative for the chapter's US regulatory content.

8. [Colorado AI Act Resources](https://leg.colorado.gov/bills/sb24-205) - Colorado General Assembly - Official source for SB24-205 (the Colorado AI Act), the first comprehensive US state AI consumer-protection law. Cited directly in the chapter.

9. [NYC Automated Employment Decision Tools](https://www.nyc.gov/site/dca/about/automated-employment-decision-tools.page) - NYC Department of Consumer and Worker Protection - Official guidance on NYC's AEDT bias-audit law referenced in the chapter; an example of city-level AI regulation.

10. [Stanford HAI AI Index Report](https://hai.stanford.edu/research/ai-index-report) - Stanford Human-Centered AI - Annual data-driven report on global AI policy, regulation, and adoption trends. Excellent source for currency in fast-moving AI law.
