# References: Business Intelligence and Analytics

1. [Business intelligence](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Business_intelligence) - Wikipedia - Comprehensive overview of BI evolution, architecture, and the descriptive-diagnostic-predictive-prescriptive analytics taxonomy used throughout this chapter.

2. [Online analytical processing](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Online_analytical_processing) - Wikipedia - Detailed coverage of OLAP cubes, dimensions, measures, and the MOLAP/ROLAP/HOLAP variants. Anchors the chapter's analytics architecture content.

3. [Star schema](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Star_schema) - Wikipedia - Clear treatment of dimensional modeling with fact and dimension tables, including denormalization tradeoffs. Foundation for the chapter's data warehouse design content.

4. The Data Warehouse Toolkit (3rd Edition) - Ralph Kimball and Margy Ross - Wiley - The definitive reference on dimensional modeling and star-schema design; the source most production data warehouses are still built from.

5. Storytelling with Data - Cole Nussbaumer Knaflic - Wiley - Practical guide to data visualization that complements the technical BI content with the dashboard-design judgment skills required by the chapter's evaluate-level outcomes.

6. [Power BI Documentation](https://learn.microsoft.com/en-us/power-bi/) - Microsoft Learn - Official documentation for one of the dominant BI tools, including dashboard construction, DAX, and data modeling tutorials.

7. [Tableau Learning Resources](https://www.tableau.com/learn) - Tableau - Free training paths covering visualization, dashboard design, and analytics best practices referenced in this chapter.

8. [Looker Studio Help](https://support.google.com/looker-studio) - Google - Documentation for Looker Studio (formerly Google Data Studio), a free option for student dashboards. Pairs well with the chapter's hands-on dashboard exercise.

9. [KPI Design Guide](https://hbr.org/2010/10/the-fundamentals-of-strategy-execution) - Harvard Business Review - Authoritative essay on selecting KPIs that drive strategy execution rather than vanity metrics. Reinforces the chapter's KPI-design content.

10. [Visualization Reference Guide](https://datavizcatalogue.com/) - The Data Visualisation Catalogue - Searchable catalog of chart types with strengths, weaknesses, and example use cases. Excellent companion when designing the chapter's required dashboard.
