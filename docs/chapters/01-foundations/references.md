# References: Foundations of Information Systems

1. [Information system](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Information_system) - Wikipedia - Comprehensive overview of information systems as sociotechnical combinations of people, processes, hardware, software, and data. Anchors the six-component model used in this chapter.

2. [DIKW pyramid](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/DIKW_pyramid) - Wikipedia - Detailed treatment of the Data-Information-Knowledge-Wisdom hierarchy with historical context and modern critiques. Directly supports the DIKI vocabulary built in this chapter.

3. [Sociotechnical system](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sociotechnical_system) - Wikipedia - Coverage of the Tavistock Institute origins, joint optimization principle, and modern applications. Reinforces why IS cannot be optimized as pure engineering.

4. Management Information Systems: Managing the Digital Firm (17th Edition) - Kenneth C. Laudon and Jane P. Laudon - Pearson - The dominant ABET-aligned IS textbook; Chapters 1-2 give a thorough treatment of the IS definition, business value, and the IT/IS distinction.

5. Experiencing MIS (10th Edition) - David M. Kroenke and Randall J. Boyle - Pearson - Source of the original five-component framework; Chapter 1 walks through hardware, software, data, procedures, and people with classroom-tested examples.

6. [What is an Information System?](https://www.ibm.com/topics/information-systems) - IBM - Concise enterprise-oriented definition with examples from hospitals, banks, and logistics. Useful complement to the academic framing in this chapter.

7. [ABET Criteria for Accrediting Computing Programs](https://www.abet.org/accreditation/accreditation-criteria/criteria-for-accrediting-computing-programs/) - ABET - Authoritative source for the CAC Information Systems program criteria referenced in this chapter, including the six required topic areas.

8. [Digital Transformation](https://www2.deloitte.com/us/en/insights/topics/digital-transformation.html) - Deloitte Insights - Industry-grade essays on automate-vs-informate-vs-transform with case studies. Pairs well with the three-level transformation model.

9. [Information Systems for Business and Beyond](https://opentextbook.site/informationsystems2019/) - David T. Bourgeois (open textbook) - Free CC-licensed textbook; Chapter 1 provides an alternative student-friendly definition of information systems and the five-component model.

10. [Goodhart's Law and Measurement Dysfunction](https://hbr.org/2019/09/dont-let-metrics-undermine-your-business) - Harvard Business Review - Article on measurement-driven unintended consequences. Connects the chapter's systems-thinking thread to the perils of optimizing one component in isolation.
